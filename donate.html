Questions

Do I get the game?

Yes — every perk tier (Credits, Become a Character, and Canon) includes a copy of the game when it releases. Think of a tip as a pre-order that also gets your name, or a custom character, woven in. The “tip any amount” option is pure support and doesn’t include a copy.

What’s the difference between “Become a Character” and “Canon”?

Become a Character puts a version of you into the living cast — a full citizen of the Valley who roams, talks and fights with a mind of its own, but lives in the background like the rest of the ~250. Canon goes much further: your character is written into the main story with its own arc, real relationships with the existing cast, a cloned voice, and an actual hand in how a playthrough can end. One lives in the world; the other helps shape it.

What does “a character with a mind of its own” mean?

Every named character in Beaver Valley thinks for itself — its own personality, goals, and reactions — and speaks in its own voice. Your character gets the same treatment: built in your likeness, given a personality you help design, and set loose to make its own decisions in the game.

Can my character be a villain? Can it die?

Absolutely, and absolutely. You can steer your character toward hero, menace, or lovable disaster. And yes — this is Beaver Valley, so it can get dismembered in the street. The upside: nobody dies off-screen, so you’ll be there to see it. (Death here is also rarely permanent.)

How do I claim my tier after tipping?

Leave your details in the Streamlabs tip message — your name/handle, the perk you want, and a way to reach you (Twitch or Discord is easiest). I’ll follow up to gather anything else, and for character tiers that’s where you’ll send a likeness reference and voice sample.

Any content limits on my character?

Beaver Valley is a dark, adult, satirical world — but I won’t build hateful, real-person-defaming, or genuinely harmful content, and I keep final creative say so your character fits the town. We’ll always land on something you’re happy with.

Refunds?

Tips are voluntary contributions to development, handled through Streamlabs under their terms, and are generally non-refundable. If something goes wrong with a perk, reach out and I’ll make it right.
