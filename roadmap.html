Where it’s going

Roadmap

The engine and its systems already run. What’s ahead is welding them into one persistent world you can save — or end. Here’s the honest order of operations.

In Progress

Building now

The Living Town of Beaver Valley

Stitching the contained modes into one persistent, breathing town where all ~250 AI characters keep real daily schedules — clocking in to jobs, drinking at the bar, serving jail sentences, running up debt, getting patched up at the hospital. The bank, repo men, jail routines, hospital staff and NPC economy are already wired in, so the world keeps living whether or not you’re watching.

Live Band Performances

The town’s bands actually play music: real tracks are split into instrument stems so drummers hit on the beat and guitarists strum in time, bringing venues like Beaver Machine’s Bar to life. Work continues on perfecting the musicians’ hand animation so every performance looks as good as it sounds.

War Mode Team Battles

War Mode is growing from a soldier brawl into full 4-versus-4 team combat, with up to four players fighting side-by-side with AI squads that draw from their own loadouts. Playable today and actively deepened with battlefield polish — depth-scaled blood, water, and ambient warscape audio.

Planned

Committed next

Murder Mystery Mode

The next standalone simulation — and the last mode still marked “Coming Soon” on the menu: a whodunit where the town’s AI characters become the suspects, witnesses, and victims. You crack the case by questioning souls who each genuinely remember their own version of what happened.

Battle of the Bands

A citywide musical showdown pitting Beaver Machine against the Stone City Orchestra and Trump’s imported British rivals. Each act performs a solo set before a chaotic all-at-once finale, with the outcome tipping the balance of cultural power in the valley.

Expanded Item & Inventory Library

An ever-growing arsenal of weapons, consumables, and absurd junk, backed by deeper inventory and item-management systems. Planned Twitch channel-point integration will let viewers drop items directly into the chaos.

Vision

The bigger ambition

Full Story Campaign & Infinite Endings

A systemic single-world campaign where destruction is permanent and no soul ever dies off-screen. Your choices steer the whole town across a spectrum of outcomes — almost every run ends differently. And the two extremes pay out in the real world: one releases a brand-new Beaver Machine album as a digital asset, the other unlocks an entirely new game mode.

Time Travel & the Beaver Eater

A one-use time machine hidden in the sewers, an ancient extraterrestrial beaver civilization, and a monstrous Beaver Eater whose fate in the past rewrites the entire present-day town. Undo its original attack and whole districts of the city simply never came to exist.

Space, Mars & the Planetkiller

Starship Mode grows into a true endgame: launch a five-station crew to colonize Mars for humanity’s survival, or watch a stolen planetkiller warhead’s transport spiral into catastrophe. Survival, permanent orbit-lock, and planet-ending disaster are all on the table.

The World State Formatter

A hidden runtime authority that governs every AI soul and can break the fourth wall — distorting the UI and audio after time travel and forcing you to talk your way back into reality with your actual voice. The final arbiter of where it all lands.

Supernatural Life & Death Systems

A full metaphysical layer of ghosts, exorcisms, once-per-game seances, cloning, and raisable zombie armies. The Reverend can pull souls from bodies and reinsert them — but a botched resurrection creates a warped, uncontrollable Abomination, because in Beaver Valley death is rarely the end.

“Beaver Tech” & Behavioral Change

Unlockable alien beaver technology that applies psychological effects to the town’s AI characters — genuinely shifting how they behave and how they interact with the world and each other. It opens up ways to change minds and resolve conflicts that have nothing to do with violence, and it’s central to steering the Valley somewhere new.

⚠
Roadmaps are promises made in smog. Order and scope can shift as the town demands. What ships when it’s ready — and in Beaver Valley, “ready” is doing a lot of heavy lifting.
